üìú Thematic Backstory: The Shattered Realm

The Kingdom of Aethelgard has known a thousand years of continuous rule, but the passing of the childless King Theron has plunged the realm into a perilous interregnum. The **Royal Keys**‚Äîfive sacred artifacts representing control over the Treasury, the Military, the Clergy, the Grand Council, and the Free Cities‚Äîhave been scattered, hidden in secure, powerful strongholds across the kingdom. You are the head of one of the Great Noble Houses: House Lionheart, House Serpent, House Stag, or House Raven. Your goal is not mere territory; it is to leverage your House's unique strengths, gather resources (**Gold**, **Food**, **Influence**), establish control over key provinces, and secure all five of the scattered keys. Only the noble who presents the five Royal Keys to the Royal Capital can be crowned the new monarch. Kingdom Keys draws its inspiration from the grand strategy and negotiation elements found in classic games like *Diplomacy* and *A Game of Thrones: The Board Game*, combined with the resource management and accessible pathway movement of games like *Ticket to Ride* or *Catan*. We aimed to create a mid-to-heavyweight strategy game where player interaction‚Äîthrough alliances, negotiation, and military might‚Äîis as crucial as efficient resource optimization and positional play on the clear, provincial map.

üí° Design Pillars

What: The Goal

The core objective is to strategically acquire five distinct **Key Tokens** representing control over key royal institutions, granting the right to claim the empty throne.

How: The Mechanism

Players achieve this goal primarily through a blend of streamlined area control on the simplified provincial map and calculated risk-taking in Action Card management and negotiations with rival Houses.

Why: The Experience

Kingdom Keys delivers a compelling experience of tense political maneuvering and emergent player narratives, where trust is a fragile resource and every move shifts the balance of power.

Trade-offs and Dilemmas

  • **Gold vs. Food:** Do you spend limited resources on offense (Gold for movement) or defense/stability (Food for sustaining armies)?
  • **Military Expansion vs. Political Influence:** Spending a turn to build an army leaves you vulnerable to a player using **Influence** to steal control of a valuable province.
  • **The Trust Dilemma:** Every alliance must eventually be broken for a player to win. The dilemma is *when* to betray an ally.

Typical Play Session

A typical full play session of **Kingdom Keys** lasts approximately **90 to 120 minutes** for 3-4 players.
